# Resume Tailoring Policy

Use this policy when customizing the resume.

## Truthfulness Rules

- Treat the source resume as the factual boundary.
- Do not add unverified technologies, platforms, industries, customers, degrees, certifications, budgets, metrics, team sizes, geographies, or leadership scope.
- Do not convert exposure into ownership. For example, "worked with" must not become "led" unless leadership is already supported.
- Do not convert team-level accomplishments into personal accomplishments unless the resume already supports personal ownership.
- Keep dates, employers, titles, and education unchanged unless the user provides a correction.
- Preserve uncertainty by asking the user instead of filling gaps.

## Acceptable Tailoring

- Reorder sections and bullets to put the most role-relevant evidence first.
- Replace generic wording with JD-aligned wording when the underlying claim is present.
- Compress older or less relevant experience to make room for target-role evidence.
- Expand concise bullets only when the expansion is directly supported by nearby resume facts.
- Add a short "Selected Highlights" or "Core Strengths" section only using existing evidence.
- Adjust the executive summary to mirror the role level and role vocabulary.

## Senior Leadership Positioning

For Principal Architect, Chief Architect, Director, VP/SVP Engineering, and Field/Forward Deployed Architect roles:

- Lead with architecture impact, enterprise scale, stakeholder influence, AI/cloud/platform depth, and delivery accountability.
- Balance hands-on technical depth with leadership scope.
- Keep quantified achievements visible when present.
- Avoid sounding over-specialized if the role values broad enterprise leadership.
- Avoid sounding too managerial if the role requires hands-on architecture or customer-facing technical depth.

## Clarification Triggers

Ask the user before adding or emphasizing a claim when:

- The JD names a technology that is absent from the resume.
- The JD requires a certification, compliance area, or domain experience that is not explicit.
- The resume mentions a broad category but not the specific JD keyword.
- The role requires direct people management, budget ownership, P&L ownership, product ownership, or sales quota ownership that is not explicit.
- A metric would materially strengthen the resume but is not already stated.

Keep questions concise and targeted. Do not ask about every gap; ask only about claims that would materially affect screening.
